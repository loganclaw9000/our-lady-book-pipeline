# Chapter 21 Retrospective

## What Worked
ch21_sc03 landed cheapest among the three (1 drafter + 2 regens + 3 critics across both passes) despite being the synthesis scene that closes the chapter. The cradle-walk does heavy work — it inventories the dead corps, plants the Ixtlilxochitl re-evaluation ("he had not chosen the winning side because it was winning"), and pivots Itzcoatl from grief-arithmetic to forward-arithmetic — but it pins to a concrete countable: *first cradle empty / second dormant / third empty / fourth dormant / fifth abandoned*. The arithmetic is visible in the mud. The historical axis (Cuauhtémoc's elevation, the bronze bell at the staging ground) and entity axis (*Sun-That-Answers*, the unnamed Tezcatlipoca, the boy attendant) interlocked cleanly because the inventory structure carried them.

ch21_sc01 also held its donts axis — the disease-as-not-a-Spanish-weapon framing ("the disease was not an arrow. It was not a spear") avoided the easy moral target without preaching. That's a hard axis to land on a chapter centered on smallpox.

## What Drifted
ch21_sc02 was the most expensive scene of the chapter — 2 drafter calls, 3 regens, 3 critics across the two passes, and crucially the only scene that required a *fresh drafter call* after chapter_critic failed (not just a regenerator pass). The drift was on the arc axis: the scene asks the reader to accept two specific callbacks ("you will come when you come" from Ixtlilxochitl + the Texcocan philosopher's "name the thing you do not want to name" callback to Otumba's *I am beginning to choose*) and synthesize them into a new named arithmetic (*I have no house*). That's three named formulas stacked into one beat. The first chapter_critic pass (6611 output tokens) flagged enough that the regenerator alone couldn't repair it — the drafter had to re-attempt.

ch21_sc01's two drafter attempts on the first pass suggest historical-axis friction on the Cuitláhuac/Cuauhtémoc transition timing — the runner-arrives-at-dusk beat compresses an 80-day reign and a succession into a single afternoon, which is canonically defensible but tight.

## Emerging Patterns
ch20's thesis ("synthesis scenes can cost more regens than introduction scenes even when introducing zero new metaphysics") sharpens here: **the cost driver is callback-density, specifically callbacks to *named formulas* from earlier chapters.** ch21_sc02 introduces no new metaphysics — engine-attends-funeral is presented as old custom, the philosopher's exercise is explicit reuse, "I have no house" is generated by an exercise the reader has seen Itzcoatl perform before. Yet it's the highest-cost scene because three named-formula callbacks must align simultaneously on the arc axis.

ch21_sc03 holds the counter-pattern: it also synthesizes (re-evaluates Ixtlilxochitl's October defection, accepts the lost war), but pins its synthesis to a literal countable inventory rather than to interior-formula stacking. Cheaper.

The chapter_critic → scene_kick recovery is informative: when the chapter as a whole fails, the system correctly targeted the synthesis-pivot scene (sc02) for full re-draft and the bracketing scenes for regen-only. The pivot carries the chapter's load and is where chapter-level coherence breaks first.
