# Chapter 04 Retrospective

## What Worked
ch04_sc02 cleared on a single critic pass (drafter → critic → next scene, no regenerator event) despite introducing an entirely new metaphysics surface — the Totonac engines with jade eye-sockets, animal masks, and a hum at "a different interval, a different harmonic, like four singers in a different mode." The entity axis (four named Totonac engines, the cacique, Malintzin, Bernardo, the four-mouth translator chain) and the metaphysics axis (non-bone or unknown-bone power source) both land cleanly, and Bernardo's "*oratio.* … *not the same word*" beat does the metaphysics-introduction work through *contrast with La Niña* rather than through assertion. The scene stages two engine traditions facing each other and lets the harmonic mismatch carry the new rule.

## What Drifted
ch04_sc01 needed a regen cycle (drafter → critic → regenerator → critic) on what is, by ch04 standards, *familiar* metaphysics — Flora's relic-box, the cord-and-medal binding ("not magic, not metaphor, but a real conducted thing, a thrum like a struck bell that travels through bone"), the pilot-weeping vs faceplate-weeping distinction. The drift surface is the historical axis stacked on top: the cabildo founding mechanics (alcalde mayor / justicia mayor / capitán general, wax warmed in seawater, three clerk copies) plus the nine-ship burn roster (Consolación, Trinidad, San Lázaro, Victoria, San Pablo, San Jerónimo, San Juan de Letrán, San Bartolomé, San Nicolás, Santa Cruz). Separately, ch04_sc03 was drafted (drafter + 1 regen + 2 critic passes in the event log) but does not appear in `assembled_from_scenes: [ch04_sc01, ch04_sc02]` — and the chapter_critic ran *twice* on ch04_sc00 (the assembly slot), suggesting the chapter-level pass forced sc03's removal.

## Emerging Patterns
The ch01→ch02 thesis ("attempt count tracks metaphysics-introduction density") does not survive ch04 cleanly. sc02 introduces more new metaphysics than sc01 reuses, yet sc01 regens and sc02 does not. The sharper read: regen tracks *axis interaction load*, not single-axis density. sc01 fuses metaphysics (Reliquary at idle, the binding cord) with a heavy historical-axis payload (Veracruz cabildo legalese, the ship-by-ship burning) and a donts-axis temptation (the inland-leaning smoke column, the keel-break of *Santa Cruz*) — three axes contending. sc02 isolates the new metaphysics inside an entity-axis frame (the cacique, the translator chain, the Totonac pilots) and lets juxtaposition do the work, so only one axis is doing heavy lifting at a time. Second pattern: chapter assembly is now a real filter — sc03 was drafted, regenerated, and accepted at scene level but cut at chapter level, which is a new failure mode the per-scene retros have not previously surfaced.

## Open Questions for Next Chapter
- Foreign-metaphysics introduction passes first-pass critic when staged as comparative juxtaposition against established rules (Totonac engines vs La Niña), inverting the ch01–ch02 thesis that metaphysics-introduction density predicts regen count.
- Chapter-level assembly can cut a scene-critic-passed scene (sc03 drafted with 1 regen, then excluded from assembled_from_scenes), implying chapter_critic operates as an independent filter axis rather than a rubber-stamp over scene-critic verdicts.
- Reused metaphysics surfaces (Reliquary binding, Flora's bones) still trigger regen when fused with new historical-axis density (cabildo founding, nine-ship burning roster), suggesting axis interaction — not single-axis density — drives regen.
