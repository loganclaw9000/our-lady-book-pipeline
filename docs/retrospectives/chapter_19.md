# Chapter 19 Retrospective

## What Worked
ch19_sc01 landed the metaphysics axis cleanly on the second regen: the "wrong hum" of *San Esteban del Río* established as a third lower than *La Niña*'s, "a tone like the bottom of a cracked bell, registered in the sternum before the ear" — a sensory hook that then threads through ch19_sc02 ("that lower third, the cracked-bell tone, lodged behind the breastbone") and ch19_sc03 ("A third lower than *La Niña*'s. Cracked-bell."). Single motif, three scenes, zero drift on the metaphysics axis after sc01 stabilized it. ch19_sc02's three-language heresy test (Nahuatl/Spanish/Latin, same sentence, leaning on it "the way a man tests a beam") fused arc and donts axes in one structural device — held in a single regen cycle despite packing Ixtlilxochitl's introduction, the brigantine arithmetic, and Bernardo's vocational pivot. ch19_sc03's Malintzin "half-second longer" stutter on the lineage-statement (Xochitl Tlapala) carried entity-continuity payload (Mexica sister, widow of the massacre) without exposition — the gesture is the reveal.

## What Drifted
ch19_sc01 took 2 regen cycles + 2 critic passes (regenerator latency 90013ms then 32395ms, critic 80372ms then 94004ms) — the heaviest regen load of the chapter. Likely cause: simultaneous introduction of new engine (San Esteban), new saint (Reconquista knight, 1491 Granada), new adorator (Fray Diego), new Latin register (Mozarabic), AND the unnamed-Tlaxcalan-pilot watching motif, all on the metaphysics + entity axes. The "Granada... a place he tried not to visit in his thoughts" beat reads as voice-correct on second pass but the first regen suggests it didn't land cleanly out of the drafter. Secondary drift risk: the "He would. That was the thing that wanted saying, and it was the only thing." refrain appears verbatim in sc01 and sc03 — deliberate echo on writer's part, but a chapter_critic that didn't flag this is either rewarding the echo or missing it.

## Emerging Patterns
**Motif-threading amortizes density.** The "third lower / cracked-bell" hum is planted in sc01 (where it costs 2 regens) and then reused in sc02 and sc03 as an anchor — both later scenes hold their voice fidelity in a single regen each despite carrying heavier historical/entity loads (sc02: Ixtlilxochitl + brigantine logistics + heresy; sc03: Itzcoatl + Obsidian Mirror + Xochitl Tlapala + Malintzin/Bernardo private meeting). This refines the ch15/ch17 thesis: it's not just regen-count tracking canon-introduction density, it's that **a stabilized sensory motif from the high-regen scene becomes a voice-fidelity subsidy for the rest of the chapter**. The cost is paid up front in sc01 and the chapter draws against it.

**Metaphysics-introduction outweighs canon-introduction for regen.** ch17 inverted ch15. ch19 partially restores ch15 but with a sharpening: when both kinds of introduction occur in the same chapter, the metaphysics scene (sc01, new engine + new saint + new liturgy) eats more regen than the canon-dense scene (sc02, Ixtlilxochitl defection + brigantine industrial scale + Bernardo's heresy). Possible reason: metaphysics-introduction must coordinate with voice (the trance/cockpit-hum register is voice-load-bearing) where historical canon can be delivered through dialogue and observation.

## Open Questions for Next Chapter
- A sensory motif planted in scene 1 (the "third lower / cracked-bell" hum of San Esteban) reduces regen pressure on later scenes that pack heavy historical/entity introductions, by giving voice a fixed anchor across content shifts.
- When metaphysics-introduction (new engine + new saint + wrong-pitch trance) and historical-canon-introduction collide in the same chapter, regen load lands on the metaphysics scene (sc01, 2 regens) not the canon-dense scene (sc02 with Ixtlilxochitl + heresy, 1 regen). Refines ch15/ch17.
- The "He would. That was the thing that wanted saying, and it was the only thing." refrain repeats verbatim in sc01 and sc03. Open question whether the chapter critic flagged it or let it pass as deliberate echo.
- Bernardo's Nahuatl→Spanish→Latin heresy test (sc02) is a structural device — same sentence, three registers, used to instrument Andrés's inability to refute. Test whether this scaffold transfers to other doctrinal-strain scenes in coming chapters.
