# Chapter 24 Retrospective

## What Worked
ch24_sc02 landed first-try (zero regenerator events; one critic pass). Notable because the scene is not low-density — it stages the explicit Bernardo/Malinalli grammar-and-theology dyad and contains the load-bearing line *"whether a willing death could be a sacrament if it was also fuel"*, which is the thematic core of the chapter. What carried it was inheritance, not invention: sc02 reuses the lamp-standing-still, the *tl* cluster correction, the hum-as-second-heartbeat, and the watching-his-hands beats already committed in sc01, and it openly anchors itself with *"They had done this in Ch 11... They had done this in Ch 20"* — pulling voice and continuity from canon rather than minting them. Voice axis steady, arc axis steady, entity axis steady because the named entities (Bernardo, Tozi, Citlal, *San Esteban del Río*) were all introduced in sc01 of the same drafting cycle and reused intact.

## What Drifted
ch24_sc03 took 2 regenerator + 2 critic cycles (the most expensive scene of the chapter). The drift load sits on the **entity** axis: the scene contains an eleven-word Nahuatl glossary (*miquiztli, teyolia, tlaocoliliztli, tlapohpolhuiliztli, tlamatcayeliztli, nacayotl, eztli, cocoliztli, mauhcayotl, tlachixcayeliztli, teotl*) plus a Latin liturgical reference (*commendatio animae*, *Proficiscere, anima Christiana, de hoc mundo*, with explicit martyr-vs-layperson variant distinction). Each of those is an entity-axis assertion that the critic has to verify against canon. ch24_sc01 also took 1 regen — likely on the **metaphysics** axis, since it is the scene that first names what *San Esteban del Río*'s monstrance bracket is and re-introduces the half-awake-engine hum-as-pressure motif at chapter scale.

## Emerging Patterns
The ch19 thesis ("motif-threading amortizes density") holds for sc02 but *does not* hold for sc03. sc03 inherits all the same motifs (lamp, hum, second heartbeat, watching hands) and still costs 2 regens. The differentiator is convergence: sc03 is doing simultaneous payoff on three long-arc threads at once — Bernardo's martyrdom construction (entity + metaphysics), Malinalli's "power within" enactment (arc, called out explicitly: *"the *power within* of Tlaxcala enacted in its hardest specific case"*), and the Andrés-says-the-prayer-six-weeks-hence foreshadow (arc). The chapter's own prose names the load: *"The convergence was complete."* Motif-threading amortizes voice fidelity; it does not amortize the critic work of validating that three threads land simultaneously without contradicting prior canon.

This refines the lineage: ch01→ch02 (introduction density) → ch15 (canon-introduction density) → ch19 (motif-threading amortizes) → **ch24 (motif-threading amortizes voice but not convergence)**.

## Open Questions for Next Chapter
- In payoff chapters, regen count tracks thread-convergence density (multiple long-arc threads resolving simultaneously) more strongly than canon/metaphysics-introduction density. Motif-threading amortizes voice load but does not amortize convergence load.
- Scenes containing in-world ritual formula lists (Nahuatl word glossaries, Latin liturgy variants like commendatio animae for martyr vs layperson) carry disproportionate critic load on the entity axis even when voice is steady.
- A midpoint scene that explicitly recapitulates prior-chapter beats ("They had done this in Ch 11... They had done this in Ch 20") and reuses pre-established sensory motifs can land first-try even at high thematic density.
