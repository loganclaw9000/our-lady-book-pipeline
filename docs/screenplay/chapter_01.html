CHAPTER ONE

Three coasts. One signal. The fleet sails at dawn.
INT. CHAPEL OF HAVANA — DUSK

Two candles. A painted copy of OUR LADY OF ANTIGUA. The flagstones are old; the dead sleep beneath them.

A LOW HUM under everything. Not sound. Pressure. The note before a glass cracks.

ANDRÉS DE MOLINA (28, lean, stone-still) kneels at the altar. Palms flat on the flagstones. Forehead beside his palms. His breath in, his breath out. The hum rises and falls with him, as if it breathes him.

A FOLDED LETTER shows at the edge of his doublet. An old seal — bishop's wax.

The PRIEST (60s, Castilian) approaches with the host. Lays a warm hand on Andrés's shoulder.

PRIEST

Corpus Christi.

ANDRÉS

Amen.

The host goes into his mouth. He does NOT cross himself.

ANDRÉS (V.O.)

You don't cross yourself in the presence of a saint.

A child's memory bleeds in: a WOMAN'S VOICE, a MOTHER's, gentle, far away.

MOTHER (V.O.)

No te santigues delante de una santa.

The priest steps back. The viaticum is done. The dead are not asked.

Andrés stands. The hum stands with him.

CUT TO:
EXT. HARBOR MOLE, HAVANA — DUSK

Light failing. Wind off the Atlantic. Eleven SHIPS at anchor in the bay, lanterns coming up one by one.

Andrés walks the length of the mole. Past Seville-carved saints set into the chapel wall. Past coiled rope and tarred timber.

And then — she.

REVEAL:

LA NIÑA DE CÓRDOBA. Fifty feet of gilded plate in a wooden cradle. A KNIGHTSHIP. A RELIQUARY. Painted eyes. Gilded arms. Her MONSTRANCE — a silver-and-crystal chest set into her chest plate — glowing softly from within. The light is the light of something preserved in crystal.

Inside the crystal, dimly: a FINGER. A TOOTH. A SMALL RIB.

The hum is louder now. He does not look directly at her.

ANDRÉS (V.O.)

Flora. Child of Córdoba. Little Flower. Child-Martyr of the Mozarabes.

(beat)

She was nine years old when they killed her. Eight hundred fifty in the year of Our Lord. Six and a half centuries in her grave. They built her this body sixty years ago. I have piloted her for eight.

A GROUP OF SAILORS at the Santa Bárbara's gangway watch him pass. One crosses himself. None speak.

CAPTAINS in steel half-armor on the quarterdeck — they watch and do not speak.

SLAVES at the loading-cradle, hauling a crate of arquebuses — they watch and do not speak.

ANDRÉS reaches the rail at the far end of the mole. He looks west. The Atlantic is very dark.

ANDRÉS (V.O.)

We are going to the Americas.

(beat)

We are going to convert the Americas.

(beat)

We are going to kill everyone who will not be converted.

(beat)

We are going to bring the Faith.

He takes a small folded letter from his sleeve — a SECOND letter, addressed in his own hand: A mi madre. Córdoba. He hands it to a YOUNG SAILOR coming up the mole with a satchel for the last dusk boat ashore. The sailor nods and goes.

Behind Andrés — at the cradle — the hum RISES. It moves through the planks of the mole. Up through the soles of his boots. Into the bones of his wrists.

LA NIÑA (V.O.)

(without speaking)

Come. Come. Come.

He does not cross himself.

He turns from the rail.

He walks back toward her.

CUT TO:
EXT. SLAVE COMPOUND — POTONCHAN, RIVER COUNTRY — DAWN (PRE-SUNRISE)

A different light. Different country. Two months east, a thousand miles south.

Wood stockade. Thatch. River loud as a river is loud. The dawn is gray.

MALINTZIN (16, Nahua, slender, watchful) wakes in a hammock in the women's hut. She does not sit up. She listens.

OUTSIDE — a man's footstep, drunk, slow — STOPS at the edge of the hut. Stays. A long minute.

Her eyes track the sound without moving her head. Her breath is silent.

The footstep turns away. Moves off.

Only then does she swing her legs down.

EXT. WATERHOLE — DAWN (PALER)

Two hundred yards from the compound. Mist on the path.

Malintzin walks with a GOURD. Refills it from the pool. Bends.

Her own face breaks apart on the water.

She does not wait for it to settle.

INT. KITCHEN HUT — DAWN

Embers. Smoke. Four WOMEN around a clay pot. Three middle-aged, faces blank, working. The FOURTH is new. CITLAL (50s, Mayan-faced but Mexica in the shoulders, heavyset, deliberate). She gestures to the open spot beside her.

Malintzin sits.

A pile of corn between them. Both with knives. They husk in silence. Citlal is fast — base, tip, one stroke each. Malintzin watches her hands. Matches her pace.

CITLAL

(in Mayan)

You are a beautiful girl.

Malintzin's hands do not stop.

MALINTZIN

(Mayan)

Thank you.

CITLAL

I was once pretty. Not beautiful. Pretty was enough. I am Citlal.

MALINTZIN

Star.

Citlal looks up. A small pause. The recognition.

CITLAL

Your mother taught you Nahuatl, then.

MALINTZIN

My father. Before.

Citlal does not pick at the word before. They work. Citlal's knife slips on a rotted husk. She SWEARS softly in a dialect Malintzin does not know. Then —

CITLAL

The boy in the field. The tall one. The overseer calls him Rabbit because he runs.

Malintzin keeps her eyes on the corn.

MALINTZIN

He is well. He works with the men. He has not been beaten this week.

A long held breath leaves Citlal — held since who-knew-when.

CITLAL

He is my sister's son.

MALINTZIN

You came with him. Last year. The raid.

CITLAL

Yes.

That is all she says. She goes back to the knife. The silence is not empty. It is chosen.

It is Malintzin who, finally, breaks it.

MALINTZIN

He told me once his aunt was a princess.

Citlal's mouth moves. Not a smile.

CITLAL

Boys say things.

MALINTZIN

Was he lying?

CITLAL

He was boasting. It is not the same.

MALINTZIN

But.

Citlal sets the knife down. Looks at Malintzin for the first time, full. The look is a measuring.

CITLAL

A girl who asks but is a girl who has already decided to know. I will tell you as much as it is useful for you to carry. No more. Carrying is heavy in this place.

Malintzin nods, once. The knife comes back up.

CITLAL (CONT'D)

I was going to be married. The marriage did not happen. The city did not survive the season in which it was to have happened. That is most of it.

MALINTZIN

And the boy.

CITLAL

Was going to be married also. To my cousin. She is somewhere. Or she is not.

She gives the small Mexica shrug — compact, finished.

CITLAL (CONT'D)

I do not ask after her. Asking wears a thing down.

OUTSIDE — a man shouts at a dog. The dog does not answer. The embers under the pot have gone white at the edges.

CITLAL (CONT'D)

(quieter, as if it were a further observation about the corn)

If anything happens to me — the boy will need a friend in this compound. Someone who speaks Nahuatl to him without making it a favor. I am asking.

A silence the length of one ear of corn. Malintzin finishes the cob in her hands. Reaches for the next.

MALINTZIN

I speak Nahuatl, and Mayan, and a little of the coast tongue. I have not been asked for anything in a year that was not a taking. I will be his friend.

Citlal nods once. Does not thank her.

We HOLD on Malintzin's hands working. Steady. Unchanged.

CUT TO:
INT. MOCTEZUMA'S AUDIENCE CHAMBER — TENOCHTITLAN — MORNING

Stone. Brazier-light. Twin shrines of HUITZILOPOCHTLI and TLALOC visible through a doorway behind the dais. Beneath the floor — felt, not heard — the GREAT ENGINE'S HUM. A low constant liturgy.

ITZCOATL (16, scribe-priest of the calmecac, brown habit, shaved temples, alert) stands honor guard at the eastern wall. His heels feel the hum. He does not move.

On a woven mat at the dais center — MOCTEZUMA II (52, turquoise diadem set straight on his brow, face worn under it). He has slept perhaps four hours across two nights. He looks at the floor more often than at the speakers.

ITZCOATL (V.O.)

I had been a scribe-priest of the calmecac for two years. I had stood guard at six audiences. I had never seen him like this.

A RUNNER kneels before the mat — dust-stained, knees torn, breath finally even.

RUNNER

The pale strangers have landed again, lord. Not the small party of last year — more of them. Their houses-on-the-water are anchored at the river the Chontal call Tabasco. Beards. Iron. Animals taller than men.

He gives NUMBERS. The numbers are larger than last year's.

Moctezuma does not interrupt. When the runner is finished:

MOCTEZUMA

How many ships.

RUNNER

Eleven.

Moctezuma closes his eyes for the length of one breath. Opens them.

A SECOND PARTY enters — QUAUHTLI (60s, eldest astronomer of Itzcoatl's calmecac, turquoise ear-spool catching brazier-light), TLACAELLE (40s), and TWO STUDENTS. They carry wax tablets.

Quauhtli kneels. Sets a report on the mat in the formal voice.

QUAUHTLI

Lord — Venus did not rise at her proper hour. The morning star has broken step by three fingers north of yesterday's mark on the floor-map of the heavens. Tlahuizcalpantecuhtli is walking early. The sun rose as he should, Huitzilopochtli kept his order — but Venus did not. This is not your sign, lord.

He pauses.

QUAUHTLI (CONT'D)

This is the Feathered Serpent's.

The chamber goes very still.

UNDER THE FLOOR — the Engine's hum does not change. The not-changing of it is its own answer.

ITZCOATL (V.O.)

The dormant Quetzalcoatl engines at Cholula and at Tula had slept for generations. No living person had seen one walk. The question on the mat — whether to send priests across the Sierra Nevada to confirm the seals — now had a date attached to it.

MOCTEZUMA

When does she set.

QUAUHTLI

Three fingers westward of Chapultepec, lord. Before sunset. We will not sleep for three nights.

Moctezuma looks tired in a new way.

MOCTEZUMA

Priests to Cholula. Priests to Tula. The runner — feed him, hold him.

(a gesture so small Itzcoatl from the wall almost misses it)

Out.

The chamber empties.

INT. CORRIDOR OUTSIDE THE PRECINCT — MOMENTS LATER

Itzcoatl follows Quauhtli out. The hum thins in the stone the further they walk from the central shrine — the way a held note thins when a singer turns his head.

Quauhtli does not look at him until they reach the threshold.

QUAUHTLI

You did not speak.

ITZCOATL

You told me to hold it until the sun was at its height.

QUAUHTLI

And the sun is not yet at its height.

ITZCOATL

No.

INSERT — DREAM FRAGMENT (FAST, GRAINED):

An OBSIDIAN SCRYING POOL. A FACE in the black water — Itzcoatl's own face. The mouth moving. The TONGUE he speaks in is not Nahuatl, not any tongue he was ever taught. He hears himself answering in it after he has already fallen silent in his own throat.

He WAKES. He smells real smoke.

BACK TO SCENE.

ITZCOATL (V.O.)

Three nights running. Quauhtli heard the dream once and ordered me to keep it from every ear, including my father's, until the sun stood at nepantla tonatiuh.

QUAUHTLI

After noon. Then you will tell the tlatoani. Not before.

ITZCOATL

He looked tired.

QUAUHTLI

He is tired. The tiredness is not the reason you wait.

They walk past a window that opens west across the precinct. The TEMPLO MAYOR stands against the morning — steps bone-white at this hour, twin shrines small at the summit against a sky still pink at its edges.

For ONE BREATH — and Itzcoatl marks the breath, because Quauhtli has taught him to mark such breaths — he feels that the stones could be taken down.

He does not say it.

ITZCOATL (V.O.)

I had been ordered to hold a dream. I held the thought with it.

QUAUHTLI

Go. Eat. Return at noon.

Itzcoatl bows his head and goes.

CUT TO BLACK.

OVER BLACK:

EXT. ATLANTIC — PRE-DAWN — SUPERIMPOSE

The hum. La Niña's hum. Building.

A SHIP'S BELL — La Niña de Córdoba's — sounds the watch.

Wind. Canvas. The fleet weighing anchor.

FADE OUT.

END OF CHAPTER ONE.
