# Chapter 17 Retrospective

## What Worked
ch17_sc01 landed the metaphysics axis in a single regen cycle despite executing the chapter's largest canonical loss event (destruction of *La Niña de Córdoba*, irrecoverable loss of Flora of Córdoba's relic at the bottom of Lake Texcoco). The drafter framed the relic-departure as consensual rather than violent — "I felt Flora go. Not silence this time. A small girl letting go of my hand in the dark" — which threaded the metaphysics axis (saint-relic bond as cycle-prayer) cleanly with the arc axis (Andrés losing the prayer that contained him) without the critic flagging entity or historical drift. The action density (boulder, gap, monstrance crack, water) did not require the regen-bloat we saw in earlier metaphysics-introducing scenes (cf. ch01_sc01, ch09).

## What Drifted
ch17_sc02 was the heaviest scene in the entire chapter by attempt count: ~10 critic invocations across four drafter/regenerator restart cycles (event log shows three full drafter restarts plus multiple regen→critic loops). This is the inverse of the prior chapter pattern — the scene introduces no new metaphysics, no new historical canon, and no new entities. It is Andrés sitting against a wall in Tlacopan re-living the three-second decision not to help Bernardo. The drift was on the arc axis: Andrés's interiority around moral failure ("I watched him for three seconds and I kept walking. The engine was not what kept me walking. I was") apparently kept failing voice-fidelity or arc-coherence checks. ch17_sc03 also drifted, though less severely — three drafter calls suggest the Reliquary-roster recapitulation (eight named engines plus losses) repeatedly broke entity-axis continuity until the drafter rewrote the ledger from scratch.

## Emerging Patterns
The ch15 thesis ("regen count tracks historical-canon density when the scene is canon-introducing") does not extend here. Ch17 inverts it: the lowest-regen scene (sc01) is the one that destroys established canon, and the highest-regen scene (sc02) introduces nothing canonical at all. The transferable pattern across this chapter's scenes is that **regen count appears to track decision-density rather than introduction-density** — sc02 contains the chapter's load-bearing moral commitment (the three seconds) and sc03 contains the chapter's load-bearing arc commitment (Andrés rising to walk toward the column without yet accepting San Esteban del Río), and both are heavily regenerated; sc01, which executes a pre-staged loss without requiring a new commitment from Andrés, is not. The entity-roster recap in sc03 (Nuestra Señora del Campeón, Santiago del Paso, San Sebastián de Cuba, Santiago del Alba, San Cristóbal del Viento, La Niña de Córdoba, Teōmecahuītlī, San Esteban del Río) appears to have driven drafter restarts rather than regen patches, suggesting roster-density requires holistic redrafts rather than incremental fixes.

## Open Questions for Next Chapter
- Regen count tracks moral-interiority density when the scene is decision-bearing — refines prior canon-introduction thesis. ch17_sc02 had ~10 critic passes despite introducing zero new metaphysics or canon, only the "three seconds" interiority.
- Arc-aftermath scenes that recapitulate the named-entity roster trigger full drafter restarts rather than regenerator passes. ch17_sc03 took 3 drafter calls (vs 1 for sc01) on the casualty/Reliquary-list section.
- Metaphysics-loss scenes converge in one pass when the lost element was established in earlier chapters. ch17_sc01 destroyed Flora's relic in 1 regen cycle because her vita was pre-loaded across prior chapters.
- Voice fidelity may strain hardest on stillness/aftermath beats rather than action beats — sc02 (sitting against a wall) regenerated more than sc01 (causeway battle).
