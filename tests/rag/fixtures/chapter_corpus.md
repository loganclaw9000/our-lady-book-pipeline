# Chapter 3: The Cholula Stir

This chapter introduces the stir at Cholula. The Spaniards have arrived in the
city and the priesthood is divided on how to respond. The feathered-breath
ledger at the Cholula temple stands at two hundred and forty grams — enough
for three major invocations or a dozen minor ones, but not enough to sustain
a prolonged engagement. This is the forcing function for the chapter's
central political argument.

Malintzin stands at the margin of the priesthood's deliberations. She speaks
both languages but is trusted by neither side. The feathered-breath politics
of the moment are not what she came here to mediate, but she sees the fault
line clearly: the priesthood will commit to an invocation strategy it cannot
sustain, and the cost will land on the people in the plaza below.

The chapter's beat function is a pre-revelation stir — it raises the tension
without resolving it, and sets up the Ch 4 betrayal that only Malintzin can
see coming.

## Chapter 3 — Scene 2: The Priest's Refusal

The head priest of Quetzalcoatl refuses to authorize the invocation. His
reasoning is entirely structural: the ledger cannot sustain what the
politicians want. He is correct, and everyone knows he is correct, and the
politicians will override him anyway. This is the scene where the reader
first understands that the empire's metaphysics and its politics have
decoupled.
