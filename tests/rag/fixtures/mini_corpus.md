# Main Rules

The metaphysics of Our Lady of Champion is deliberate, not decorative. Every rule
here is load-bearing on the plot. When a character invokes the Name, the invocation
has a cost, and the cost is always paid by someone. The narrative never permits
free lunch on any axis — political, spiritual, or structural.

Quetzalcoatl's feathered breath is not a metaphor. It is a measured substance,
consumed in grams per invocation, recorded in the ledgers of the temple-priests
at Cholula. When the substance runs out, the feathered breath goes silent, and
the empire loses a key advantage in its negotiations with the Spaniards. This is
the first binding rule of the metaphysics.

The second binding rule is that relics do not travel. A relic taken from its
cradle loses its potency within three days. This sets up the siege dynamics —
the Aztecs cannot simply send relics to reinforce a distant front, and the
Spaniards cannot capture-and-carry; they must contest the cradle itself. Every
major combat engagement in Acts 2 and 3 hinges on this rule, whether the
characters understand it or not.

The third binding rule is the Ledger of Names. Any name spoken aloud in a
sacred precinct is recorded. Any recorded name can be invoked against the
bearer, once, as a binding. This is the rule Malintzin violates in Chapter 14
when she gives Cortés a Nahuatl name he did not earn. The consequences unspool
across the next five chapters.

## Rule Cards — Hypotheticals

These are hypothetical scenarios, NOT canonical rules. The critic must treat
these as examples of how the system could behave, not as authoritative facts.

Hypothetical 1: if a priest of Tlaloc invokes Quetzalcoatl's name in a
precinct sacred to neither god, what happens? In theory, the invocation fails
silently — no cost, no effect, no ledger entry. This is the loophole Cortés'
translator exploits in a hypothetical Ch 19 variant we did not pursue.

Hypothetical 2: if a Spanish priest could somehow desecrate a cradle without
the cradle's guardian noticing within three days, the relic inside would
persist indefinitely. No character in the actual novel discovers this path,
but it is mechanically available.
